Overview

Midlothian Code is a local Virginia nonprofit organization that reaches out to those learning a programming language or seeking mentoring and networking. Midlothian Code emphasizes free, accessible solutions, such as the online learning paths Free Code Camp and The Odin Project, among others, and seeks to make more guided options available for those who seek them.

After encountering frustration over lack of functionality in existing web services, they chose me to develop a new digital home that could offer the features they needed. Based on an understanding of Midlothian Code's organization type and goals, I recommended implementing a responsive solution using a content management system for ease of updating events, information, and fundraising products.

I partnered with Midlothian Code to design and develop a responsive website that met the needs of its base.

The Challenge

At this point, Midlothian Code was a fledgling organization that had not yet registered as a nonprofit and utilized services like Meetup and Mobilize.io as its primary communication mediums. Midlothian Code needed a partner who would create a web presence that facilitated communication, customized existing resources to suit the organization, and show restraint with costs. Their timeline was two to four weeks.

The Process

Before beginning work on the website itself, I first ensured Midlothian Code had a clean, recognizable brand identity. A truly custom brand was above the organization's means, so I sought out a more automated solution that I could still guide and tweak to meet their needs.

Logo

Simplicity was sought in the logo, as well as a sense of character, since the village of Midlothian has its own rich history. Despite its simplicity, the organization's purpose is clear.

Typography

Much of the character of the logo comes from the typographical choice. The font used, Brothers, designed by John Downer gives off a strong, retro feel. It is paired with a clean sans-serif font which suits the modernity of coding.

Color

When looking at the primary color, I preferred a welcoming orange hue that would help usher in new users. This works well as orange is complemented by blue tones, a common indicator of technology-related subjects.

For the website itself, it was clear from Midlothian Code's needs that a powerful platform like WordPress was the best fit. Since completely custom work was outside the scope of this project, I researched suitable themes and instead customized an existing one to suit them. This allowed me to focus on giving functionality like eCommerce, event management and job listings.
